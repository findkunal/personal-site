This is meant to be a small, deliberate corner of the internet for the things I actually think about: philosophy, technology, leadership, and the practices that hold them together. Nothing more ambitious than that, and nothing less.

## What I'll write about

Four things I keep returning to:

- **Philosophy that has to survive a hard week.** Stoicism, Vedanta, the parts of Buddhism that aren't selling anything.
- **Technology, from inside the work.** The messy decisions, the systems that almost worked, the times the right answer was less code, not more.
- **Leadership without the theater.** How you actually decide. How you say the hard thing. How you stay honest when the room wants confidence instead.
- **Mindfulness, the practical kind.** Less retreat, more daily habit. A way to catch your own mind in the act before it carries you off.

These look separate. They aren't. The Stoic question, *what is mine to control?*, is the same question a senior engineer asks before a launch and a leader asks before a hard conversation. I'll draw those lines where I see them.

## What this won't be

Not a tutorial site. Not a hot-takes feed. Not a place where I pretend to be more certain than I am. If I write about something, it's because I've been chewing on it, and I'll say where my thinking is still incomplete.

If this sounds like your kind of thing, stick around. I'd rather build a small room of people who read than a large one that scrolls past.
